👋 Hi, I'm Blanka! Welcome to my blog!
---
        
I am a (permanent) research scientist at [Centre National de Recherches Météorologiques (CNRM)](https://cnrm.sedoo.fr/), Météo-France, based in Toulouse, France. My research sits at the intersection of machine learning and physical simulation: how can we make climate models faster and more accurate with AI?

I work primarily with the global atmosphere model [ARP-GEM](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.00829), where I design and implement neural networks to represent subgrid-scale physical processes, particularly deep convection and turbulence. I also have a growing interest in ocean modeling.

Working with ML inside a large climate model written in Fortran has naturally led me to explore heterogeneous HPC environments, including hybrid CPU/GPU simulations and MPI-based coupling between Fortran and Python applications such as [(py)OASIS](https://oasis.cerfacs.fr) and [PhyDLL](https://phydll.readthedocs.io/en/latest/).

Research is full of dead ends, unexpected bugs, and hard-won solutions that never make it into a paper. This blog is my space to document all of it: the successes worth celebrating,  the failures worth learning from, and the technical rabbit holes that took way too long to climb out of. You'll find posts on practical issues I've encountered building hybrid ML/physics models, how I solved them (or didn't), and broader reflections on the physical evaluation of AI-driven climate models and the promises and limits of physical modeling with AI.

When I'm not coding, you'll probably find me out (trail) running!
